New Curation

Social Installation, 6m x 6m x 6m

 

Inspired by my MFA graduation work, I want to propose to a new audience an accessible method of displaying large-scale Oil Painting.

Throughout history we have been forced to enjoy very large paintings with physical discomfort. 

Oftentimes we wish to be with a painting for an extended amount of time, hours even. And return to it later, for days. 

The feeling a painting of large magnitude can provide a person is usually enjoyed to the point of discomfort. 

We need to use binoculars sometimes to see the details at the top, experience neck pain, and feel frantic in the limitations of the gallery experience.

So my solution to this is to suspend W A Y shape paintings at an angle from the ceiling using 4 or 5 points,
and place a large soft seat in front, so as to be able to maximally enjoy the experience of artwork with minimal strain on the body.
